What You'll Learn

Curriculum Breakdown

Six comprehensive modules taught by domain specialists with years of early childhood experience.

  • Developmental milestones from infancy to age 6
  • Emotional and social behavior mapping in early years
  • Observation habits for classroom decision-making
  • Play-based pedagogy and language-rich routines
  • Montessori-inspired and inquiry-based techniques
  • Designing weekly plans with measurable outcomes
  • Parent briefing formats and progress sharing
  • Difficult conversations with empathy and clarity
  • Building a home-school support loop
  • Classroom rhythm, transitions, and behavior guidance
  • Environment zoning for safety and exploration
  • Inclusive practices and support strategies
  • Sensory, literacy, motor, and social activities
  • Material selection and low-cost learning setups
  • Theme-based planning for monthly curriculum cycles
  • Observation-based assessment methods
  • Developmental milestone tracking
  • Portfolio documentation for young learners
  • Identifying and supporting children with special needs

Program Timeline

  • Child psychology, ECE principles, classroom orientation
  • Teaching methods, curriculum mapping, activity planning
  • Live practice sessions, parent communication, reflection logs
  • Supervised placement, portfolio defense, final certification

Mode of Learning

Hybrid format - live online sessions twice a week combined with in-person classroom observation and supervised practice. Recorded sessions available for flexible review.

Certification

Government-recognized certificate in Early Childhood Education (ECE) issued upon successful completion of all modules and the final assessment portfolio.

Class Size

Deliberately limited to 20 participants per cohort to ensure every student receives personal attention and mentorship from our instructors.
